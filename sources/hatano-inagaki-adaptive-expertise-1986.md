# Hatano & Inagaki (1986) - Two Courses of Expertise

## Citation

Hatano, G., & Inagaki, K. (1986). Two courses of expertise. In H. Stevenson, H. Azuma, & K. Hakuta (Eds.), *Child Development and Education in Japan* (pp. 262-272). Freeman.

**Link:** [APA PsycNet](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/1986-97669-017)

## Type

Book chapter (theoretical)

## Key Insight

Distinguished between routine expertise (efficient but inflexible) and adaptive expertise (flexible, can handle novel situations). Adaptive expertise requires understanding principles, not just procedures.

## Relevance

AI use can build routine expertise (pattern matching) while eroding adaptive expertise (principled understanding). The distinction matters for professional valueâ€”adaptive experts remain valuable when contexts change.
