# Reber & Schwarz (1999) - Fluency and Truth Judgments

## Citation

Reber, R., & Schwarz, N. (1999). Effects of perceptual fluency on judgments of truth. *Consciousness and Cognition*, 8(3), 338-342.

**DOI:** [10.1006/ccog.1999.0386](https://doi.org/10.1006/ccog.1999.0386)

## Type

Paper (experimental)

## Key Insight

Demonstrated experimentally that statements presented in easier-to-read formats are judged as more likely to be true. The effect occurs automatically and unconsciously.

## Relevance

Foundational evidence for fluency bias. Explains mechanism by which polished AI output bypasses critical evaluationâ€”it literally feels more true because it's easy to read.
