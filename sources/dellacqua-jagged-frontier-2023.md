# Dell'Acqua et al. (2023) - Jagged Frontier

## Citation

Dell'Acqua, F., McFowland III, E., Mollick, E., Lifshitz-Assaf, H., Kellogg, K., Rajendran, S., Krayer, L., Candelon, F., & Lakhani, K.R. (2023). Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier: Field Experimental Evidence of the Effects of AI on Knowledge Worker Productivity and Quality. *Harvard Business School Working Paper*.

**URL:** [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4573321)

## Type

Paper (field experiment)

## Key Insight

AI capabilities are "jagged"â€”uneven across tasks. 758 BCG consultants showed 40% quality gains inside AI's capability frontier but 19pp worse performance outside it. Consultants struggled to identify the boundary, suggesting metacognitive skills are essential.

## Key Findings

- Inside frontier: +12.2% tasks, +25% speed, +40% quality
- Outside frontier: -19pp correct solutions
- Leveling effect: Low performers +43%, high performers +17%
- Two patterns: Centaurs (task division) and Cyborgs (integration)

## Relevance

Landmark study establishing that AI collaboration requires judgment about capability boundaries, not just tool proficiency.
